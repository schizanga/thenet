On [January 15th, 2026](samchizanga.com/kumba) a former friend and one-time collaborator made a TikTok about me and the party we put on, called kumba. This is not a call out or a call in. I'm not seeking a mediaiton nor forgiveness. Take it or leave it.

TL:DR -  I was called a user, opportunist, vulture, and lord knows what else. I'm not here to rehash the details of that video because for all its embellishments, the impact was hurtful. I was humiliated to my core. I would not recommend reading comments written by your omnipresent exes and/or aspiring exes if you ever do get one of these. 0/10. 

One thing is certain and you can ask in your group chats; I'm going to say _it_, whatever _it_ is. The only decision I regret was apologizing. Y’all have worked on group projects. The cost has been undeniable impact to my reputation but that is a cost but I am willing to pay. I'll find my solace in knowing who I am and verifiable truths. That said - I can verify that they are no longer associated with kumba. That was true. 

We move.

That day (January 15th) was nestled in-between other events which were calling awareness to how I viewed myself and my self-worth. I suppose that awareness had always existed but this time there was a different clarity. Growth is rarely linear, I still find myself in valleys of self-doubt and constantly question if I and the things I spend my time on have any value. These days, much less, which is why I can confidently make decisions on who I do or don't work with.

I have built many a thing on behalf of other people, creating tangible value. Long hours, massive projects, thousands of dollars raised always for next to no credit. That sounds like I was always the victim and I was, but chat? Much of that victimization was self-inflicted. 

Would you believe me if I told you that July 2025 marked the beginning of my Saturn square, a month before I invited anyone to work on that gd party? I did not have a good Saturn return and so the lessons were good and plenty. Not me thinking my return was about quality and output of my work? In hindsight, I realize that doesn't make much sense but when you're self-abandoning you tend to believe all the lies you tell yourself. 

Because the truth is the work is good. I'm an incredibly creative person and capable in so many ways. You're reading this post on a website I built with my two lesbian hands, for crying out loud.

With time and distance I’ve realized that this event, like many other events in the last 5 years, was part of an overdue and necessary lesson - I can be hard-headed. I have repeatedly chosen creative, romantic, and business partnerships where I am in service to the ego of the other, rather than the actual work which is a problem because I want to do something impactful through said work. I was functioning so that I could be liked and accepted IN my career, not to be helpful or useful - which is how I’d repeatedly end up in situations with people who undervalue **me**, don’t show up, take credit for my work. 

And why would they? I clearly was not valuing my own work, someone might as well value it for me.

My interest in astrology was loosely about reflection at best and has now become part of my process of individuation and self-actualization. What I create is a reflection of my will and my work but my work cannot speak to my character; how I show up in relationships I’m in does. When I allow myself to show up as myself without seeking approval it allows the relationships that are meant for me and I for them to exist. 

As my self-worth deepens so has my inability to withstand imbalanced relationships of all kinds. When your self-worth is based on being liked you tend to be less direct about things that will make you unlikable. I was told it wasn't what I said, but how I said it. That I was too direct. In order to maintain sovereignty **you** must make decisions that cause disruption to what threatens it, regardless of how it is received. That's the cost of doing business.

Sometimes it’s ending a long-winded phone call with a person who dominates the conversation, other times it’s choosing to end unfulfilling connections regardless of the material loss. Will it always blow up in your face? No. Can it though? Yes. Trust me when I tell you; eating the discomfort of what is not being said to maintain any connection is manipulation and worse than that is a slow poison to the health of ones self-being. Don't poison the well. 

Did I have anyone in my corner? No. Will that stop me? Also no. Am I still whole? Yes. I have tended to my wounds, talked to the moon, realized who the fuck I am (again), and this time it might stick. 

Sam
